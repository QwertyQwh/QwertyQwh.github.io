To understand what a hyperbolic space is, we define distances between two points slightly differently from its usual Euclidean notion.

Let \(k\) denote a field of characteristics different from 2 and \(\mathbb{E}\) a vector space over \(k\) of finite dimension \(n\). A quadratic form on \(\mathbb{E}\) is a function \(Q: \mathbb{E}\to k\) homogeneous of degree 2, i.e. $$Q(\lambda z) = \lambda^2 Q(z); $$ for any \(\lambda \in k, z \in \mathbb{E}\).
